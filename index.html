Why Kebab Delivers

Six hard-hitting reasons to eat more of it.

💪

High-Quality Protein

A classic doner or shish kebab packs 25–35 g of lean protein per serving, essential for muscle repair, satiety, and metabolic health.

🌿

Herb & Spice Antioxidants

Cumin, sumac, turmeric, and parsley — staples of kebab marinades — are rich in polyphenols that fight oxidative stress and inflammation.

🫀

Heart-Friendly Fats

Lamb and chicken trimmed for kebab contain a favorable mono-unsaturated fat profile. Olive oil marinades add an extra cardioprotective boost.

🥗

Fiber-Rich Accompaniments

Wrap it in wholemeal flatbread and pile on the salad: tomato, cucumber, red onion, and cabbage add gut-friendly prebiotic fiber.

🦴

Bone-Supporting Minerals

Red meat kebabs supply heme iron, zinc, and phosphorus — nutrients critical for bone density, immune function, and oxygen transport.

🧠

B-Vitamins for the Brain

Lamb and beef are among the best natural sources of B12 and B6, vitamins essential for neurological health and reducing homocysteine levels.

The Kebab Family

Every style has its own nutritional strengths.

🌯

Doner

High protein, great for post-workout recovery. Choose chicken for a leaner cut.

🍢

Shish

Lean grilled chunks — minimal fat loss in cooking. The athlete's choice.

🥙

Adana

Spiced minced lamb. Rich in iron and zinc with metabolism-boosting chili.

🌯

Souvlaki

Greek-style pork or chicken. High in B vitamins, served with probiotic tzatziki.

🍢

Seekh

South Asian minced kebab, grilled on skewers. Loaded with aromatic anti-inflammatory spices.

Tips for a Healthier Kebab

Small swaps, big nutritional wins.

🍢

Go Shish Over Doner

Shish (skewered chunks) tends to be leaner than pressed doner. Ask for extra salad and go easy on creamy sauces.

🥛

Yogurt Sauce > Mayo

Swap garlic mayo for tzatziki or plain yogurt. You get probiotics and calcium without the saturated fat hit.

🌾

Wholemeal Wrap

A wholemeal flatbread instead of white lowers the glycemic load and adds an extra gram of fiber per serving.

🌯

Load Up the Salad

More cabbage, tomato, and onion means more fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants. Don't be shy with the greens.

🫒

Olive Oil Marinade

Ask for meat marinated in olive oil and lemon — it keeps it moist, slashes oxidation during grilling, and adds polyphenols.

🥙

Skip the Chips

Trade the side of chips for extra tabbouleh or a fattoush salad. You'll halve the calorie count and double the micronutrients.
